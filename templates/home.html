Data & Visual Analytics Final Project: The A Team

In January of 2021, the retail investors of r/wallstreetbets coordinated to act against short selling interest of institutional investors. This action was unprecedented in financial market history and researchers are just beginning to analyze it. What follows is The A-Team's analysis of the sentiments of posts on r/wallstreetbets and the price of the stocks they discussed.

What impact did WSB Sentiment & Mentions
have on stock price?

The first of our next two graphs shows how sentiment and stock price changed over the relevant time period. Our final graph uses a neural network to test if sentiment was a good measure for predicting price.

GME Stock Price & Sentiment over Time

Below is a graph depicting how the price of GameStop and the sentiments of comments on WSB changed over time. Stock price is the real price every 15 minutes. Sentiment is a 2 hour rolling average at each 15 minute point. Our assumption is that the past 2 hours of sentiment has an impact on the current price. Sentiments greater than 0 indicate positivity and sentiments less than 0 indicate negativity.

You can filter the graph by the amount of days you want to look at and by which time period. You can also toggle trendlines.

Do GME Sentiment & Mentions predict GME Price?

Below are the results of 28 Artifical Neural Network runs tuning a number of different features. The results are sorted by minimum RSME which stands for Root Mean Squared Error. The best run just barely edged out our base model which was tuned with only financial indicators. The best run included the financial indicators as well as the last 8 hours of GameStop mentions from WSB. Other than that single run, no one combination of mentions, sentiment etc beat out our base model. So we conclude that there was a correlation between the last 8 hours of mentions of GameStop on WSB and GME's price that could beat just financial indicators. However sentiment did not have good predictive power.

A description of the features is as follows:

  • WS/NS: With Sentiment/No Sentiment

  • MA: Moving Average

  • SD: Standard Deviation

  • RSI: Relative Strength Index

  • WR: Williams %R

  • s0/s8/s24: Time lagged sentiments (e.g. s8 = past 8 hours)

  • 0mntn/8mntn/24mntn: Time lagged mentions (e.g. 8mntn = past 8 hours)

  • sgd/adam Neural Network Algorithm

  • pos/neg/neu Postive/Negative/Neutral sentiment (if empty and has a sentiment timelag, only compound sentiment included)
